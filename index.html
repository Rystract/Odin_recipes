Odin Recipe

Description

Lasagna is a wide, flat sheet of pasta. Lasagna can refer to either the type of noodle or to the typical lasagna dish which is a dish made with several layers of lasagna sheets with sauce and other ingredients, such as meats and cheese, in between the lasagna noodles.

Originally, the word lasagna was used to describe a pot in which food was being cooked rather than the pasta itself. Lasagna recipes have been around for ages, dating back to as early as the thirteenth century. Today, lasagnas come in all kinds of varieties from veggies and cream sauces to traditional sauce and cheese lasagnas as well as meat lasagnas.

Ingredients

  • Bechamel sauce with cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Olive oil
  • Onions
  • Crushed Garlic
  • Beef mince lean
  • Chopped Tomatoes
  • Dried Origano
  • Lasagna sheets
  • Parmesan cheese Finely grated

    • Steps

    1. Heat oil in a large frying pan. Add onion and garlic and cook for 5 minutes, until softened, then add mince and brown well.
    2. Pour in the tomatoes and sprinkle over the dried oregano. Simmer, stirring occasionally, for 10 -15 minutes, until thickened. Season with salt and pepper, to taste.
    3. Preheat oven to 180 C.
    4. Grease a medium oven proof baking dish. Line base with ¼ of the lasagna sheets, trimming to fit if necessary
    5. Spoon 1/3 or the mince mixture over the lasagna, cover with a generous layer of béchamel sauce.
    6. Repeat the layering twice.
    7. Cover béchamel with a layer of lasagna sheets, and spread over the remaining béchamel.
    8. Sprinkle over the cheese and bake for 30-40 minutes, until the pasta is tender and cheese is golden brown.
    9. Cover with foil if necessary during baking, to prevent cheese from burning.
